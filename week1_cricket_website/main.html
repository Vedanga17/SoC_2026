What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard pitch. The game originated in south-east England in the late 16th century.

The main objective is to score more runs than the opposing team. One team bats, trying to score runs, while the other team bowls and fields, trying to dismiss the batters and limit the number of runs scored.

Game Highlights & Legends

Cricket has produced some of the most memorable moments in sports history:

  • The ICC Cricket World Cup: The biggest tournament in the sport, held every four years. Australia have the most titles, with 6.
  • Legendary Players: Sachin Tendulkar (India), Sir Don Bradman (Australia), and Sir Viv Richards (West Indies).
  • Formats: The game has evolved from 5-day Test matches to 1 day ODIs to fast-paced 3-hour T20 matches.

How to Get Started

You don't need much to start practicing in your backyard! Here is the basic gear:

  • A Cricket Bat: Usually made of willow wood.
  • A Ball: A hard leather ball for professionals, or a tennis ball for beginners.
  • Wickets: Three wooden stumps placed at each end of the pitch.

Beginner Tip: Start playing with a tennis ball to practice hitting and catching safely before moving to a hard leather ball.
